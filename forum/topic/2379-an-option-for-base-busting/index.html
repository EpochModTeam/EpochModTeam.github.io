        Personally I don't think video games should reward tedious/grindy game play, like in World of Warcraft, or in Epoch where one might spend 30-60 minutes hitting a wall with a hammer or hatchet to get the loot inside a base. I realize others find this rewarding, or to be the source of the meaning behind their effort, but I think there are better ways. Also I understand bases can be busted very quickly with the right guns, too quickly, too easily in my opinion, this idea would also address that.

        Â 

        How about the only way an upgraded cinder block wall (or some further advanced wall) can be destroyed is by something like a C4 charge, that costs... 100 gold, or whatever you (dev/admin) think appropriate, and it can only destroy one wall. That way, serious base busting is something you have to be sure you want to commit to, not just something you can roll around doing for several hours at several different bases absorbing loot.

        Â 

        I understand with the next patch armed vehicles will reset with zero ammo and ammo will spawn rarely. I'm not sure what will happen, its definitely an improvement, I just assume people will now loot cycle the barracks for hours, or just use sledgehammers, both of which seem to reward people who have a talent for completing tedious tasks that take little thought. Not saying these people are dimwitted, just as I said before, from a game development perspective I wouldn't want to encourage menial tasks, life is too full of those already.

        Â 

        If you took the time to read my entire post thank you for that and any thoughtful criticism is welcomed, but please don't just post a meme response, or short sarcastic comment, please.
