        [2015-09-25 | 21:10:11] Player #8 Player name (IP Information) connected
        [2015-09-25 | 21:10:12] Player #8 Player name - GUID: xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx (unverified)
        [2015-09-25 | 21:10:12] Verified GUID (xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx) of player #8 Player name
        [2015-09-25 | 21:10:12] Player #8 Player name - Owner GUID: xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx

        Everyone else logged in like this......

        [2015-09-25 | 21:03:16] Player #4 Player name (IP Information) connected
        [2015-09-25 | 21:03:17] Player #4 Player name - GUID: xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx (unverified)
        [2015-09-25 | 21:03:17] Verified GUID (xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx) of player #4 Player name
        [2015-09-25 | 21:03:18] Player #4 Player name - Legacy GUID: xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx

        Here is the best way to find out if someone is hacking. check the age of the steam account.  If the player is a lower level and has his profile on private, watch him like a hawk.  Hackers have to inject past BE. The current way they do it is: they stop or pause BE so they get a lot of BE timeouts and get kicked.  The legacy GUID should be because of having your ArmA account before they change over from gamespy to 100% steam.  Newer accounts probably don't have the legacy tag. Hackers are not going to risk their main account.

        if you want to check steam ID # and what their GUID # is, go here: http://codepen.io/micovery/full/bNbLqL  converts steam IDs into GUID #
