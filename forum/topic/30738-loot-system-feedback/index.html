        Running around looting today as usual I started to notice some fairly annoying issues with the current loot system. The way I understand it, as you are walking the loot system detects the direction you are moving & tries to spawn loot ahead of you. While this sounds good in theory, and works fairly well in groups, solo looting can be extremely tedious, & here's why:

         

        The idea behind the system seems to assume your character will be walking in a straight line, but when actually playing this is very rarely the case in my experience. I usually run building to building taking back alleys where I can & trying to stay as invisible as possible. Also running back & forth inside buildings & up stairs to grab said loot seems to confuse the system as to what direction you are heading.

         

         

        This has cause multiple instances where I've had to sit inside a building for up to 10 minutes waiting for the loot to actually spawn. In one particularly bad instance, I stayed at & searched the same barracks 4 times before the first piece of loot actually spawned. In another instance, I was providing overwatch for my friend as he looted what we call "Industrial Lane"(the main road heading north out of Kavala). After he finished looting roughly 3km of houses, warehouses, barracks, & more, he was frustrated at how little he had found. I had to explain to him that he was moving too fast for the loot system, & that he needed to wait for the loot to spawn, When I think about it though, I'd rather not have to worry about whether or not I'm "looting correctly" while I'm playing. It's kind of immersion breaking. :(

         

        I'd love to give some ideas for a better loot system that isn't overly demanding for the server or client, but I have little knowledge of such things, & don't even fully understand how how the current system works. These are just some things that I noticed on my adventures in this awesome mod you guys have made! Hopefully this will be helpful & not a waste of your time.

         

        Keep up the good work! :)
