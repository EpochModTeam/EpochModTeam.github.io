        What was the reason behind removing linkage between humanity and player appearance?

         

        My main concern is that I can no longer effectively hunt down bandits, since there is almost no way to identify them, usually the only way to make sure is to camp their trader, which is not fun. I don't want to shoot folks that are just minding their own business, so killing everyone is not an option, and I'm trying to become a hero as well. Gaining humanity through bandit kills in the next version would be nearly impossible without proper identification. Currently it seems to me that many bandits are getting away from me, since I let them pass, because I don't know who they really are.

         

        While it's probably impossible to create a realy foolproof humaity-skin system, a not very good one is still better than nothing. I find it fair that depending on player appearance I can provide them with what they are looking for: a fight or some friendly interaction. Of course there are cases of hostile survivors and friendly bandits, but generally humanity system saved my life more than was the reason of my defeats.

         

        It's nice to see many wearable skins, but I wish that default ones would reflect humanity again, and the rest would be restricted to different kinds of players depending on their humanity. Also default bandit skins could be split into at least 2 categories: from 0 to -5000 and from -5000 till whatever max value is, to distinguish between actual bandits and folks who just defended themselves and will regain their humanity later. I know that there is already a "nametag" system, but it works really bad as a mean of identification due to small distance and ability to not show your name.

         

        Judging my personal experience, there seems to be a lot more hostility between players compared to other mods. I have a lot less friendly encounters during my adventures here, in other dayz mods probably at least half of them used to not end with us shooting each other. There are many people who panic upon meeting with other players, in Epoch they seem to join the "shoot on sight" crowd completely, since they have no idea if there is a bloody murderer in front of them or just some friendly guy. That results in more hostility and paranoia, I think there's already enough of that.
