        This idea came to mind during a base raid last night that featured my group (two of us) and another group (5 of them). We were able to communicate enough and divide teams up for cover and assault that no one was killed, but this kind of system would have helped out immensely:

        Â 

        Currently, the group system is pretty solid. The more money you invest, the more members you can have. This is great, and keeps base security working great.Â 

        Â 

        An Alliance system could be an optional benefit for smaller crews teaming with larger crews for raids. Currently, all those involved in last night's raid play on Mells House, which is 100 players at peak time. If we were able to ally up and have different indicators (from the normal hexagon) on ally bodies (triangles, for example), we would know who is who during the tense moments. There was a lot of delay in calling out targets due to normal confusion.

        Â 

        Potentially, this system could be something that expires after 24 hours so that every time crews want to team up, they have to pay a krypto fee. The only perk to allying is less confusion during a raid or PVP encounter. It also allows players to team up without compromising their base security. Playing alone (which some players tend to hate to do) could be less of an issue too.

        Â 

        I know this isn't something that everyone would utilize, but it would make bigger raids a thing that certainly has less confusion. Less confusion means less apprehension to try it.Â 

        Â 

        TL;DR: Make a system that players can pay for to allow temporary alliance, different body indicators, only perk is temporary indicators, allows for larger raids, allows solo players to try out playing with crews, expires after a short time. Thoughts?
